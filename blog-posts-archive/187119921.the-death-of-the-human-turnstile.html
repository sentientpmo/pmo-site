The traditional PMO is a relic. It is a department of Archaeology—specializing in digging up the past to report on why a project died last week.

If you want to survive the shift to Algorithmic Assurance, you have to stop acting like a “Human Turnstile” and start acting like a Sentient Architect.

Here is the Friday Audit. If your team is doing the things on the left, you are paying a massive Admin Tax. If you want to move to the right, you have to change the telemetry.

STOP Doing These 5 Things Today:

  1. Stop acting like a “Human API.” If you are paying a senior leader $160k/year to manually move data from a meeting into a spreadsheet and then into a PowerPoint, you are misusing your assets. That is “middleware” work. Bypassing the human is the first step to clarity.

  2. Stop “Emotional Babysitting” (The Shock Absorber). Stop hiding the “Red” to keep the peace. When you “smooth over” a vendor’s failure, you aren’t helping; you’re an enabler of bad behavior. You are trading your Credibility (Gold) for a temporary “Green” square.

  3. Stop “Compliance Theater.” Checking a box to see if a document was uploaded isn’t governance. It’s a Boolean check. If your PMO is obsessed with the artifact instead of the integrity of the data inside it, you are a “Human Turnstile.”

  4. Stop relying on “Implicit Trust.” In my time at Illumio, we secured 6 of the top 10 banks because we assumed the network was breached. Why do you assume your project is safe? Never trust the status report. Always verify the telemetry.

  5. Stop performing “Archaeology.” If your Weekly Status Report tells me what happened last Friday, it’s useless. I don’t need a historian; I need a Navigator who can see the iceberg before we hit it.

START Doing These 5 Things Today:

  1. Start building the “Project Brain” (The Refinery). Wire up your data sources. Ingest the “Digital Exhaust”—the transcripts, the commit logs, and the emails. Turn the raw noise of the project into a structured signal.

  2. Start performing “Forensic Linguistics.” Monitor the verbs. When the vendor shifts from “We will” to “We are evaluating,” confidence has collapsed. Don’t wait for the missed deadline; audit the Linguistic Decay in real-time.

  3. Start “Auditing the Silence.” Look for the Silence Signal. If your Go-Live is 30 days out and the team has stopped talking about “UAT” or “Testing,” they are lost in a fire. The things they aren’t saying are your biggest risks.

  4. Start calculating “Zombie Spend.” Every week a critical risk sits in “Evaluating” status, money is leaving the building. Calculate the Enterprise Value Erosion. When you show a leader that “politeness” just cost them $1.5M in shareholder value, the “Truth Gap” closes instantly.

  5. Start leading with “Gold and Bitcoin.” Build your Credibility (Gold) by telling the unvarnished truth early. Then, use your Courage (Bitcoin) to stop the line. A PMO that doesn’t have the guts to kill a “Frankenstein Plan” isn’t a governance office; it’s a cost center.

The “Paper Generals” will stay in the Left Lane because it’s safe. It’s quiet. It’s polite.

But the Sentient Architects are already in the Right Lane. We’ve wired the telemetry. We’ve installed the agents. And we’ve stopped paying the Tax.

Never Trust. Always Verify.

— Michael Kaplan, PMP
