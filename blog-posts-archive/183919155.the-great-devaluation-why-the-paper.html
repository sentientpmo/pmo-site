  1. A sea of “Open to Work” green badges.

  2. People celebrating: “I just got my PMP!” or “I just got my Scrum Master cert!”

And I see 50 people liking it. “Congrats!” “Way to go!”

We need to stop with the toxic positivity. You are cheering for someone who just bought a ticket to the Titanic after it hit the iceberg.

The “White-Collar Recession” reported by LinkedIn News isn’t a temporary dip. It is a permanent structural correction. The market is ruthlessly eliminating the “Middlemen” of the corporate world.

Why I Am Warning You (The Progenitor’s Guilt)
I am not an outsider throwing rocks at the castle. I helped build the castle.

I have been building PMIS systems before the acronym existed. My first system was for the Pentagon Renovation in 1995, built from scratch in MITRE’s Lab. I worked with the pioneers of this industry—Joel Koppelman (Primavera) and Dick Ferris. Later, at Morgan Stanley, I was the one who pushed the first integration of MS Project data into SharePoint.

I know what is happening now because I know what we built then. We intended to build systems of Control. Instead, we built systems of Tax. We gave corporations the ability to micro-manage complexity without understanding it.

I helped build the prison. Now, I am handing you the key.

Here is the hard truth about why the profession is contracting, and why those jobs are never coming back.

1. The Credential Bubble Has Burst

For twenty years, the “Credential Industrial Complex” (PMI, etc.) committed the ultimate economic sin: They devalued their own currency.

To drive revenue, they prioritized Volume over Excellence. They convinced millions of people that if they just memorized 49 processes or learned the inputs and outputs, they were “Project Managers.”
They flooded the market with “Paper Generals.”

  • Supply: Infinite.

  • Demand: Shrinking.

  • Result: Senior leaders—Architects with 20 years of experience—are now forced to compete on price with “Bootcamp Grads” because HR algorithms can’t tell the difference.

Your certification is no longer career insurance. It is a receipt for a tax you shouldn’t be paying.

2. The Collapse of the “Agile Industrial Complex”

For the last ten years, I have watched the Agile community throw stones at Project Managers. They told us “Manager” was a dirty word. They told us that if we just bought enough sticky notes and “trusted the team,” the software would write itself.

They were arrogant. And they were wrong.

While they were mocking the PMP, they were busy building the single greatest act of bureaucratic hypocrisy in history: SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework). They took the worst parts of the “Admin Tax” and industrialized it.

Now, the market is handing them their credentials to eat.
Agile was invented to solve Human Latency (people are slow). In 2026, AI writes and tests code instantly. AI removes the latency.
If you are still getting paid $140k to facilitate a “Daily Standup” or ask “What did you do yesterday?”, you aren’t managing flow; you are enforcing a ritual.

The “Ceremony Masters” are being evicted. The ranks are closing. And frankly, it is long overdue.

3. The “Jig Is Up” on the Admin Tax

For years, I managed a $25M NERC CIP portfolio—one of the most regulated environments on earth.
By “Standard” metrics, that role requires 60 hours a week of grinding.
I did it in 20.

I didn’t cheat. I delivered Zero Audit Findings.
I simply refused to pay the “Admin Tax.” I automated the governance. I built a machine that did the chasing, the reporting, and the logging for me.

Corporations are realizing they have been paying full-time salaries for roles that are 80% administrative waste.

  • If your value is “updating the file,” you are gone.

  • If your value is “facilitating the meeting,” you are gone.

From Physics to Fluff (and Back Again)

I started my career as a Master Scheduler for the Pentagon Renovation.
In that world, Governance was Physics. If the schedule was wrong, the blast-resistant concrete didn’t get poured. You couldn’t “status report” your way out of a missing wall.

Somewhere along the way, our industry lost that rigor. We replaced “Physics” with “Process.” We started valuing the artifact (the chart) over the asset (the outcome).

The current contraction is painful, but necessary. It is the return of Physics.

The Choice
The “Sea of Green Badges” isn’t going away until we admit the truth: The era of the Administrator is over.

But the era of the Architect is just beginning.
The market is starving for leaders who know how to:

  1. Diagnose complex systems (Forensic Governance).

  2. Architect automated workflows (The Zero-Tech Stack).

  3. Drive strategic pivots based on data (The Gold Line).

It is time to hit the “Escape Button” on the old way.
Stop banking on the badge. Start building the machine.
