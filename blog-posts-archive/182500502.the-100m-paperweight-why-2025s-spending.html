In my last analysis, I talked about the “False Efficiency” of 2025—the year we tried to replace logic with language models.

But there is a second, more expensive shoe about to drop in 2026. It’s a crisis of Capital Expenditure (CapEx) realization.

In 2025, corporations went on a historic shopping spree. Driven by FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and the AI hype cycle, companies poured billions into “Hard Assets”:

  • NVIDIA H100 chips.

  • New Data Centers.

  • Grid Modernization hardware.

  • Enterprise Software Licenses.

The receipts are in. The assets are on the books. GDP recorded the growth.

But as we look at the start of 2026, many of those assets are sitting idle. They are Stranded Assets.

Why? Because companies bought the equipment, but they fired the people who know how to plug it in.

The “Gym Membership” Fallacy

Corporate strategy in 2025 fell victim to the “Gym Membership” fallacy.

It’s the belief that buying the equipment creates the result. It’s like spending $5,000 on a Peloton and a squat rack, firing your personal trainer to “save money,” and then wondering why you haven’t lost weight six months later.

CapEx (Buying the thing) is easy. It just requires a checkbook.
ROI (Getting value from the thing) is hard. It requires behavior change, workflow integration, and governance.

The “Project” wasn’t buying the server. The Project was rewriting the business workflow to use the server without crashing the company. That work hasn’t even started yet.

The “Last Mile” Problem

In the industries I operate in—Critical Infrastructure, Energy, and Finance—this gap is even more dangerous.

You can build a $50M substation or deploy a massive AI model, but you cannot just “turn it on.”

  • The Compliance Gap: A Generative AI model cannot self-certify for NERC CIP or FDA GxP. If you turn it on without a human Governance Architect verifying the boundaries, you are inviting a federal audit.

  • The Integration Gap: The new AI tool has to talk to the 30-year-old legacy mainframe. That doesn’t happen via magic; it happens via rigorous API orchestration and testing.

  • The Adoption Gap: If the operators on the floor don’t trust the new system, they will bypass it.

Without the “Middle Layer” of program leadership to manage these three gaps, that $50M asset is just a very expensive paperweight.

The 95% Failure Rate

We are seeing data that 95% of AI pilots fail to reach production.

This isn’t a technology failure. It’s a Governance Failure.

It is easy to get a chatbot to write a poem. It is incredibly difficult to get that same chatbot to query a proprietary, regulated database and give a financial answer that won’t get you sued.

The companies that succeed in 2026 won’t be the ones who spend more on Compute. They will be the ones who spend more on Control.

The Fix: The Return of the Integrator

This is why the job market is about to correct.

Boards are going to ask CEOs: “We spent the capital. Where is the return?”

To get the return, companies will have to bring back the Integrators.

  • The Governance Architects who build the firewalls.

  • The Program Directors who manage the vendor interfaces.

  • The Change Managers who retrain the workforce.

We are not here to “manage the spend”—that money is already gone. We are here to manage the activation.

You bought the Ferrari. It’s time to hire the driving instructor.
